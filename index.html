Welcome to My Gran Canarias Trip

Introduction

Welcome to my travel blog about my amazing trip to Gran Canaria Island! This beautiful island, part of the Canary Islands, offers stunning beaches, vibrant culture, and unforgettable experiences. Join me as I share my journey, from the golden dunes of Maspalomas to the historic streets of Las Palmas. Discover the beauty and adventures of my trip to Gran Canaria Island. From stunning beaches to vibrant culture, join me as I share my experiences and memories from this incredible journey.

Trip Itinerary

  • Day 1: Arrival and exploring Las Palmas
  • Day 2: The Cathedral of Arucas
  • Day 3: Beach day at Playa del Ingles and the Dunes
  • Day 4: Visiting local markets and tasting Canarian cuisine
  • Day 5: Exploring natural pools
  • Day 6: Night view in Galdar

Trip Highlights

When we arrived on this island we found a strange paradise. That's how I've described it because it's something different, strangely beautiful. The vibrant streets of Las Palmas were bustling with life, offering a mix of modernity and tradition. The architecture was a delightful blend of colonial and contemporary styles, making every corner a visual treat.

The Cathedral of Arucas represents a unique architectural style in the history of Spanish architecture. This magnificent structure, built from volcanic stone, stands as a testament to the island's rich cultural heritage. The intricate carvings and towering spires are a sight to behold, and the interior is equally breathtaking with its stunning stained glass windows and ornate altars.

Playa del Ingles in the back of the Maspalomas Dunes is a beach destination that truly has it all, whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore the natural beauty of the dunes, or experience the vibrant nightlife. The golden sands stretch for miles, and the dunes create a surreal landscape that feels like a desert by the sea. The beach is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports, while the nearby promenade offers a variety of restaurants, bars, and shops.

Tasting delicious Canarian cuisine at local markets and restaurants. The local markets are a feast for the senses, with fresh produce, aromatic spices, and a variety of traditional dishes. From papas arrugadas (wrinkled potatoes) with mojo sauce to fresh seafood and tropical fruits, the flavors of Gran Canaria are both diverse and delightful. The friendly vendors are always eager to share their culinary secrets and offer samples of their delicious fare.

The natural pools are coastal areas marked off with breakwaters and volcanic stone, they are watertight spaces fill and empty themselves at low and high tide without any help from human's hands. These pools offer a unique swimming experience, with crystal-clear waters and a serene atmosphere. They are perfect for a refreshing dip, snorkeling, or simply relaxing by the water's edge while enjoying the stunning coastal views.

Last day we had a great night in Galdar. Gáldar is a town and a Spanish municipality in the north of the island of Gran Canaria in the Province of Las Palmas in the Canary Islands. The town is known for its rich history and vibrant culture, with ancient cave paintings, historic buildings, and lively festivals. The night was filled with music, dance, and local delicacies, making it a perfect end to our trip.
